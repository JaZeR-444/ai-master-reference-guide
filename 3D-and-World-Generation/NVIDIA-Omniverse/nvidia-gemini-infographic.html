6. The "Solo Builder" Friction Test

A critical analysis of time expenditure for a solo developer. While Omniverse excels at large-scale assembly, it introduces significant overhead for rapid prototyping compared to traditional game engines.

✅ When to Invest (High Leverage)

  • ▸
    Multi-Tool Pipelines: You use 3+ tools (e.g., Blender, Houdini, CAD) and waste time importing/exporting.
  • ▸
    Synthetic Data Needs: You are training computer vision models and need physically accurate ground-truth data.
  • ▸
    Industrial Clients: You are selling services to manufacturing, logistics, or robotics firms.

❌ When to Avoid (Low Leverage)

  • ▸
    Standard Game Dev: You are building a standalone executable game (Unity/Unreal are still superior for packaging).
  • ▸
    Low-Spec Hardware: Requires significant VRAM (RTX 4080/5080+) for smooth operation.
  • ▸
    Solo Stylized Art: Overkill for simple low-poly or non-photoreal projects.
